Teams often prototype AI features as isolated experiments: custom chat boxes, custom loading states, custom disclaimers, custom failure patterns. That accelerates demos and destroys product coherence.

Users do not evaluate one AI widget in isolation—they evaluate whether the whole product feels trustworthy. Consistent interaction primitives matter more than novel animations.

## The minimum AI design primitives

- unified confidence/result framing
- consistent retry and fallback affordances
- shared citation/evidence UI pattern
- shared partial-response and streaming behavior
- shared failure language for model/tool outages

When these are centralized, you ship faster because teams compose patterns instead of reinventing them.

## Takeaway

Sleek motion and modern visuals are great—but consistency is what turns them into trust instead of novelty.
